WISDOM HAT

Once upon a time, there was a very old king who wanted to pass the throne to his only son who was somewhat stupid.
But the courtiers and the people opposed that idea. They suggested to the king that six months later he held a talent contest.
If the prince cannot prove his wisdom, he will not be crowned.

The king was very worried, could not think of a good way, so he sent the prince to see a witch. He said to the prince:

- You must give this to the witch. She is a magical person, if she eats your food, she will happily impart magic to you.

The prince remembered the king's words, he brought a lot of food, he ate it while walking, and he also gave his food to everyone he met on the road.
Finally the prince met an old woman, he brought the remaining dried fruit to her. The old woman took it, the prince asked, indeed she was the witch.
So the prince begged her to give him wisdom.

The witch said:

- I am glad to give you wisdom. But on condition that the king closes all schools, because schools give people knowledge, they will rob me of my witchcraft.

The prince hastily agreed.

The witch knitted the prince a hat of wisdom, when wearing that hat, everyone would become extremely intelligent.
The prince returned, told the story he had experienced, the king was overjoyed. They believe that the prince will definitely inherit the throne.

The day of the talent contest came, the people sent nine young men to give speeches with the prince.
Although it was very hot, the prince still wore that hat. He was a very successful speaker and was applauded by many people.

The prince's intelligence surprised everyone, and the hat he wore was also very strange.
Therefore, a smart person recommends, when giving a speech, to stand up straight and take off your hat to show respect for everyone.

This time the king had no way to refuse.
The second speaking contest begins.

The prince took off his hat of wisdom and spoke with a humiliating awkwardness.
Everyone laughed and forced the prince to lower the stage.

In the end, they chose one of the smartest of the nine young men to be king, the remaining eight were all ministers.
